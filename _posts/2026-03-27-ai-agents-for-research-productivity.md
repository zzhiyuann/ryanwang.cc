On **March 27, 2026**, I gave a lab share session on how I use AI agents in day-to-day research and engineering work.

The core argument was simple: the useful shift is not just "better chatbots." It is learning how to treat AI agents as an operational layer for real work. In practice, that means persistent memory, tool access, review loops, role separation, and interfaces that let humans stay in control.

The session covered three concrete threads:

1. **Research productivity**: using agents to summarize literature, review writing, and automate repetitive analysis workflows.
2. **Personal infrastructure**: building assistants that persist context across sessions instead of starting from zero each time.
3. **Multi-agent systems**: organizing specialized agents with review and approval layers so they can do useful work without becoming opaque.

I also walked through examples from my own stack, including persistent memory, automated QA layers, and a team-style operating model for agent collaboration.
