<div class="page-intro">
  <p class="page-lead">My research develops <strong>ubiquitous sensing and intervention systems</strong> to enhance human health and well-being. I work at the intersection of mobile and wearable computing, machine learning, and clinical science through collaborations with medicine, nursing, psychology, and bioethics.</p>
</div>

<div class="focus-grid">
  <div class="focus-card">
    <p class="focus-card-kicker">Digital Health</p>
    <h3>Wearable sensing systems for health monitoring and intervention.</h3>
    <p>Designing real-world sensing pipelines that can capture meaningful behavioral and health signals outside the clinic.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="focus-card">
    <p class="focus-card-kicker">Behavioral AI</p>
    <h3>Multimodal approaches to understanding and predicting human behavior.</h3>
    <p>Modeling affect, social context, and intervention opportunities from speech, mobile sensing, and digital diaries.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="focus-card">
    <p class="focus-card-kicker">Human-Centered AI</p>
    <h3>LLM-enhanced health systems with stakeholder participation.</h3>
    <p>Building health AI systems that keep clinicians, patients, and ethical constraints in the loop from the start.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="focus-card">
    <p class="focus-card-kicker">Affective Computing</p>
    <h3>Detecting anxiety, affect, and social context from everyday signals.</h3>
    <p>Turning ambient mobile and speech signals into interpretable, clinically relevant measures of lived experience.</p>
  </div>
</div>
